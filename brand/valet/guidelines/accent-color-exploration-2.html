Accent color exploration · Round 02

FINALISTS

Old Bronze · Deep Slate · Depth before commitment

Round 01 surfaced four candidates; two earned the second look. Round 02 goes deeper on each: the exact anchor within the family, the companion dim derivative for reduced-emphasis contexts, an honest contrast check against the house dark stack, wordmark rendering across the production size ladder, a chrome sampler that shows how the accent behaves in product UI, and a narrative of what selecting each locks in downstream.

Read this document in parallel, not in sequence. Every section pairs Bronze on the left and Slate on the right; the eye should hop across, not down. The goal is not to prefer a candidate in the abstract but to see the product that each becomes.

01

The two families

Why each made the cut
A OLD BRONZE Warm · Classical · Tactile Anchor #8A6B3D · oklch(0.48 0.07 80)

Bronze leans into the material register of the brief — the tactile warmth of handled objects. A brass fitting on a bound portfolio. The warmth of age, not the shine of new metal.

The register is unambiguously classical. The risk is adjacency to house amber; this round confirms whether Bronze reads as a family member or a variant of the parent.

B DEEP SLATE Cool · Formal · Considered Anchor #3E5366 · oklch(0.37 0.04 240)

Slate carries the discipline of professional formality without tipping into institutional coldness. The color of record — ink on cooled paper, the back of a leather daybook.

The risk is visibility: the anchor sits close to the house dark stack. This round tests whether the mark holds its own against dark or asks the icon geometry to do all the work.

04

Contrast reality

WCAG against the dark stack

WCAG 2.2: 3:1 minimum for UI components and large text (18pt+); 4.5:1 minimum for normal body text. The house dark stack ranges from #0A0A0A (base) to #1A1A1A (elevated). Each candidate is tested below.

A Bronze · #8A6B3D
on #0A0A0A VALET 3.94 : 1 AA Large
on #111111 VALET 3.65 : 1 AA Large
on #1A1A1A VALET 3.31 : 1 AA Large
Verdict: usable as-is. Bronze at the anchor clears the 3:1 threshold for UI components and large text on all three house background values. It does not reach 4.5:1 normal-text — but accents are not body text, and for wordmark / icon / button labels, the anchor is compliant without modification.
B Slate · #3E5366
on #0A0A0A VALET 2.49 : 1 Fails 3:1
on #111111 VALET 2.30 : 1 Fails 3:1
on #1A1A1A VALET 2.09 : 1 Fails 3:1
Verdict: anchor requires lifting for UI use. Slate at S·03 fails the 3:1 threshold for UI components on every house background. For the wordmark and decorative contexts (large-scale marketing surfaces, splash, hero) this is acceptable — the mark is an object, not UI — but for favicon, active UI, buttons, and links the anchor must shift upward. S·04 (#526878) clears 3:1 on all backgrounds; S·05 (#667E8D) clears 4.5:1. Selecting the Slate family likely means authoring two-anchor tokens: a visual-only "deep slate" for decorative surfaces and a brighter "active slate" for UI.

07

Downstream consequence

What selecting each locks in

The accent is one decision; the product's visual language follows. What does the whole become, if this is the color?

A If Bronze
The product becomes

A well-tended room in a house you have kept for a long time.

The rest of the brand resolves warm. UI neutrals tint toward earth rather than blue. Iconography favors engraved-plate register. Marketing imagery (if any) prefers leather, wood, binder-cloth textures — the materials the brief called for.

  • Typography pairs with transitional or old-style serifs (the voice-serif exploration will confirm — candidates will skew classical)
  • Icon forms can lean tactile — engraved plate, threshold, door fitting
  • Copy register tips toward understatement; the warmth does rhetorical work
  • Market differentiation is strongest — nothing in the consumer-AI space looks like this
  • Risk to navigate: keeping the execution contemporary, not costume
B If Slate
The product becomes

A well-kept daybook on a quiet desk.

The rest of the brand resolves cool, precise, ledger-register. UI neutrals tint toward the same blue. Iconography favors clean architectural geometry. The product feels closer to Archon in spirit — considered, engineered — but personal rather than institutional.

  • Typography pairs with contemporary serifs or grotesques with narrow counters (the voice-serif exploration will confirm)
  • Icon forms can lean geometric — ledger marks, ruled lines, formal structure
  • Copy register tips toward precision; the cool formality carries the tone
  • Market differentiation is less strong — cool dark accents are common in developer tools; distinctiveness comes from the register, not the hue
  • Risk to navigate: the two-anchor token system (S·03 decorative + S·04 functional) to maintain WCAG compliance
Decision format: (1) which family — Bronze or Slate — and (2) which tonal point within that family (e.g. B·03 or S·04). If Slate, also confirm the two-anchor token approach. Once selected, the icon exploration renders its candidates in the chosen color and this round closes.
