THREE FORMS

I·01 · I·02 · I·06 · Deeper examination of each

Three finalists from Round 01. Each is a distinct architectural grammar — The Post (a pair of verticals), The Binding (a stack of horizontals), The Ledger (a line carrying an entry). This round examines each along three axes: geometric variants within the candidate's form, wordmark pairing at common layout sizes, and surface context across the house dark stack and the accent surface itself.

Each plate carries a highlighted anchor variant — the current best expression of that direction — plus two alternatives that show the refinement space. Pick a plate. Then pick a variant within it. Or request a Round 03 with tighter iteration on one direction.

I·01 THE POST Principal & Attendant
a Geometric variants · axis · attendant height & offset

The Post's character is defined by how the attendant stands relative to the principal. Taller attendant reads as peer; shorter reads as deferential. Closer reads as companion; further reads as staff.

a·01 Close companion
Gap 12 · Attendant 30 px (75% of principal) Closer pair, more equal heights — reads as companion, still clearly secondary. The tightest relationship.
a·02 Anchor · deferential
Gap 18 · Attendant 26 px (65% of principal) The Round 01 anchor. Balanced distance and height — at post, at hand. Classical servant-to-principal proportion.
a·03 Formal distance
Gap 24 · Attendant 22 px (55% of principal) Further, shorter — reads as staff-at-a-discreet-distance. Most formal proportion in the set. Risks reading as two disconnected marks.
b Wordmark pairing · icon + wordmark at common layouts

Icon and wordmark rarely appear together in production — the icon is for favicon and app-icon use, the wordmark for headers and titles. But they must coexist on login screens, social preview cards, and attribution surfaces. Four typical layouts below.

The Post reads as what the Valet paradigm is — a principal and an attendant, present together. No threshold to cross, no frame to fill, no record to accrete. Just the pair, at post.

Across the three variants, a·02 (the anchor) holds the classical proportion most faithfully. a·01 softens the hierarchy — the attendant reads as peer, which is contemporary and warmer but less register-true. a·03 formalizes the distance but risks the two marks reading as unrelated rather than in relationship.

Strongest at: paradigm match — the mark literally is the product's thesis. Weakest at: memorability outside the register — without the Valet framing, the two verticals are minimal to the point of asking viewers to read intent into them.

I·02 THE BINDING Bookbound · Record · Continuity
a Geometric variants · axis · bar width distribution

The Binding's character is defined by how the three bars relate in width. Equal widths read as ledger or hamburger menu; varied widths read as bookbinding marks or a monogram. The anchor uses narrow-wide-narrow.

a·01 Anchor · bound
Widths 20 · 32 · 20 The Round 01 anchor. Narrow-wide-narrow reads as bookbinding on a spine — the widest bar is the center gather, the shorter outers are head and tail bands.
a·02 Cascade
Widths 36 · 28 · 20 Wide to narrow top-to-bottom — reads as a compositional foot, like a block of text with a tapered bottom. More dynamic, less symmetric.
a·03 Accent bar
Widths 20 · 36 · 20 · center stroke 3.5 Thicker center bar emphasizes the gather. More weight, more register-specific (bookbinding marks often have a heavier center band).
b Wordmark pairing · icon + wordmark at common layouts

The Binding reads as a thing that has been kept — the material evidence of pages gathered and bound. For a product whose central promise is continuous memory, the form is almost too literal: it is the physical object that a ledger of one's life would be.

Among the variants, a·01 (the anchor) is the cleanest bookbinding reference. a·02 introduces typographic dynamism but tilts the register from bound toward composed. a·03 amplifies the anchor by thickening the center band — register-accurate to actual book spine gathers, but risks the icon reading as heavy at favicon scale.

Strongest at: material register — nothing in the house has this form, and the bookbinding association is available without being pointed out. Weakest at: the 16 × 16 reading can collapse into a three-bar hamburger-menu silhouette if the viewer doesn't register the width variation.

I·06 THE LEDGER Entry on a Line · Temporal Record
a Geometric variants · axis · entry position & count

The Ledger's character is defined by the relationship between the line and the mark. A single offset entry reads as a moment of attendance; two entries read as a sequence; a centered entry balances formally but loses the temporal asymmetry.

a·01 Centered entry
Line 48 px · block 9 × 9 · centered Balanced, formal — the mark on the middle of the line. Reads as present rather than recorded over time. Loses the temporal narrative of the offset variant.
a·02 Anchor · offset entry
Line 48 px · block 9 × 9 · offset right (x=36) The Round 01 anchor. Reads as an entry made recently on a long line — temporal, accretive, continuous. The asymmetry does the narrative work.
a·03 Two entries
Line 48 px · two blocks 7 × 7 · offsets x=18, x=40 Two marks read as a sequence. Stronger temporal register — explicitly multiple moments — but the smaller blocks may fail at 16 px. Most explicit paradigm match.

The Ledger reads as a line of time with a mark on it — continuity + presence. It is the candidate most closely aligned with the product's explicit differentiator: continuous personalization with temporal awareness. The offset block says something happened here; the line says this has been going on a while.

a·01 (centered) is formally balanced but collapses the narrative — the mark on the middle of the line is present without suggesting continuing. a·02 (the anchor) carries the temporal reading through asymmetry. a·03 (two entries) makes the sequence explicit — strongest paradigm match, weakest favicon legibility.

Strongest at: paradigm differentiation — this is the only candidate that explicitly encodes time. Also the only candidate combining stroke + fill, giving it a distinctive textural reading among the sibling icons. Weakest at: 16 × 16 — the filled block may compress to 2 × 2 pixels, approaching the visible threshold.

WHAT EACH SERVES

Paradigm fit summarized
I·01 The Post
Serves · Relationship
Literal rendering of the Valet paradigm: a principal, an attendant, steady at hand. The mark is the product's thesis. Strongest register match.
Trade Simplest element count. Memorable only if the viewer supplies the register — two verticals are minimal enough that the relationship must be inferred. The brief does the work.
I·02 The Binding
Serves · Continuity
Material rendering of a thing that has been kept. Bookbinding is available as a reading without being pointed out. The strongest material-register match to the brief.
Trade Three horizontal bars risk reading as hamburger menu at favicon scale if the width variation isn't preserved through rasterization. Must verify 16 × 16.
I·06 The Ledger
Serves · Temporal awareness
Explicit encoding of time as substrate. The line is a day; the mark is an entry made. Only candidate that carries temporal awareness — the actual product differentiator.
Trade Only candidate using stroke + fill — distinctive, but the block may fail favicon scale at 9 × 9 scaled to 16 × 16 canvas. May need to be lifted to 10 × 10 or 11 × 11.
