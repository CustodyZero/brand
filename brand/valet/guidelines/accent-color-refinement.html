B·03 · B·04

Two anchors. One decision. Same family.

The family is Bronze. The remaining question is exactly which bronze. B·03 sits at oklch(0.48 0.075 80) — the deeper, more patinated anchor. B·04 sits at oklch(0.54 0.075 82) — the lifted, more present variant. The difference is six percentage points of lightness and two degrees of hue. It decides whether the brand reads as a plate that has hung for decades or a plate freshly installed.

Three things separate them: accessibility, register, and amber proximity. This round shows all three honestly so the pick is informed rather than aesthetic.

02

Register nuance

What each actually evokes
B·03 evokes

The brass that has hung

The deeper anchor reads as time in service. The brass plate on a door that has been opened every day for thirty years. The patina is not decoration — it is evidence.

B·03 asks the product to earn its place slowly. It suggests continuity has already happened, before you arrive. The emotional register is reassurance: this has been here. It will remain.

The risk is recession — the color is deep enough that at small scales it can lose against the dark stack without the icon geometry carrying it.

B·04 evokes

The brass that has been set

The lifted anchor reads as in current service. The seal freshly struck. The plate that has been polished this morning. Still classical, still tactile — but active.

B·04 asks the product to carry its presence forward. Continuity is in progress. The emotional register is readiness: this is here now, attentively, and will continue.

The risk is amber-adjacency — the higher lightness brings B·04 closer to the house amber in perceived weight, even though hue keeps them distinct.

Amber proximity

Clearance from the house accent

House amber #D4880A is the closest sibling to any bronze candidate. Both anchors placed alongside amber to confirm Bronze reads as a family member, not a variant of the parent.

AMBER #D4880A · House
B·04 #9D7E49 · oklch L 0.54
Reading the strip: both bronzes clear amber on hue (bronze sits at 80–82°, amber at 60°) and on chroma (bronze chroma 0.075, amber chroma 0.14 — nearly double). B·03 is further from amber in lightness; B·04 closer. Neither will be confused with amber in direct comparison, but B·04 in isolation may read as a muted amber to someone not viewing both. B·03 has no such risk.
∙

The decision

What tips the choice

Both anchors work. Neither is wrong. The choice is a values question, not a technical one — and the values are clearly legible in the trade.

B·03 holds the classical register most faithfully. It is the color of lived-in service — the brief's material register rendered without compromise. The cost is accessibility: it holds UI-component contrast but not body-text contrast; its usable surface is wordmark, icon, chrome. The downstream UI resolves with the accent kept ceremonial rather than everyday.

B·04 holds the register slightly less tightly — it is a brass still in current service rather than one that has weathered — but in exchange it earns broad functional usability: body-text contrast on the darkest surface, UI-component contrast everywhere, greater presence at small scales. The downstream UI can use the accent anywhere the user needs to see it, without a secondary lifted tone.

The question for selection: is the brand willing to constrain where the accent appears in exchange for the deepest register, or does the brand want the accent to be broadly usable with a small softening of register?

PICK B·03 If values tip toward register
The classical material register without compromise Accessibility held to wordmark, icon, and chrome only Accent used sparingly — kept ceremonial, not everyday Cleanest separation from house amber Requires discipline downstream — where the accent appears, and where it does not
PICK B·04 If values tip toward reach
Broadly usable — body text, links, UI, chrome, wordmark AA Normal (4.5:1) on two of three house backgrounds Register softens slightly — polished rather than patinated Marginally closer to amber in isolation, still distinct in hue/chroma Simpler downstream — one anchor token covers all contexts
