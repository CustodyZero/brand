ICON MARK EXPLORATION — ROUND 2

Beyond geometric frames
Why we're moving away from frames: Round 1 explored square perimeters with interior details (dots, crosshairs, gaps). The honest problem: at favicon size (16px), every variation collapsed to "red square." The differentiating detail vanished. The frame was the least interesting part of every mark and the only thing that survived. We need marks with distinctive silhouettes that hold at every size.
Concept G
SQUARED ZERO

A squared-off typographic zero with slashed center. No curves — pure CustodyZero geometry.
The zero is the shared identity across the product house. The slash distinguishes it from the letter O and from a generic rectangle. The squared form (no border-radius) is a CustodyZero house constraint applied to the letterform itself. Reads as a specific character at every size — the slash survives 16px. Direct, typographic, zero ambiguity about what it represents.

Taller, narrower zero (Bebas Neue proportions) with a center dot instead of a slash.
Proportions echo the display typeface. The center dot replaces the slash — reads as "zero with a station inside it" rather than "slashed zero." More metaphorical. But the dot has the same 16px survival problem from Round 1 — at favicon size this becomes a plain rectangle.
Concept I
CONVERGENCE
64px
32px
16px
Four diagonal lines converging from corners toward a center square node.
Everything reports to a central point — the station. The lines are inbound, not outbound (convergence, not broadcast). The gap between lines and center creates breathing room. Distinctive silhouette: at any size, the X-like shape with a center block is recognizable. Risk: might read as a generic "connect" or "network" icon.
Concept J
ZERO BLOCK

32px
16px
Filled zero glyph — heavy stroke weight, slashed, solid presence.
The heaviest typographic approach. This is the zero treated as architecture — thick walls, defined interior void, diagonal brace. Impossible to misread at any size. The slash and thick frame both survive 16px. Boldest mark in the set. Reads unmistakably as "zero." Risk: may feel too heavy or too close to a literal font glyph rather than a designed mark.
Concept K
SZ MONOGRAM
64px
32px
16px
StationZero initials as a geometric monogram. S and Z side by side.
Directly spells the product abbreviation. The geometric construction (all straight lines, square caps) fits the house style. The S and Z have complementary shapes — S steps down-right, Z diagonals down-left, creating visual counterpoint. Risk: too much detail for 16px — the S especially will become illegible. Monograms also feel corporate rather than infrastructural.
Concept L
GROUNDED NODE
64px
32px
16px
Solid square node with ground symbol below and antenna line above.
The electrical ground symbol is literal: this system is grounded, local, physically connected to your infrastructure. The antenna line says "receiving." Together: a grounded receiver. Reads as infrastructure schematic — not decorative, functional. The vertical axis is distinctive. Risk: the ground lines may be too fine at 16px. Also reads more "electrical engineering" than "home automation."
