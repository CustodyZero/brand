ICON MARK EXPLORATION

StationZero — 64x64 canvas, Signal Red #C04848

Design Constraints

  • 64x64 canvas, must be legible at 16px (favicon)
  • No surveillance cameras, eyes, lenses, or shields
  • Must imply: local custody, defined boundary, active monitoring
  • Signal Red (#C04848) only — monochrome
  • Architectural, not illustrative
  • Must read as "infrastructure" not "security company"

Square perimeter with gap at bottom-right corner. Center filled circle.
Perimeter = defined boundary. Gap = actively monitored opening. Center dot = local processing station. Clean and geometric. But may read as "incomplete square" rather than intentional gap at small sizes.
Concept B

Complete square frame with interior crosshair (short arms, gap at center).
Reads as a registration mark / alignment target — precision infrastructure. The crosshair implies calibration and focus without being a literal camera reticle. Square frame = boundary. Crosshair = the system is locked on, paying attention. Strong at all sizes.

Two concentric squares. Outer boundary, inner core.
Layers of containment — the outer perimeter is the property line, the inner square is the station itself. Reads as "system within a system." Minimal, architectural. Risk: might read as generic "frame" or UI element rather than an intentional mark. At 16px the inner square may lose distinction.
Concept D
CORNER BRACKETS

32px

Four corner brackets defining a zone, with a center dot.
The brackets define a zone without enclosing it — the boundary is implied, not drawn. Reads as a viewfinder or registration zone. The center dot is the station. Strong associations with precision, targeting, focus area. Risk: could read as a camera viewfinder (too close to surveillance imagery?). But also reads as architectural markup / crop marks.
Concept E

Solid red square with a dark square void cut from the center.
Inverts the typical approach — the red IS the boundary (solid, present, covering). The void at the center is the station: the protected interior space. Reads as solid infrastructure with a defined interior. Very high contrast. Boldest mark in the set. Risk: large color footprint — Signal Red dominates.

The off-center node breaks symmetry intentionally — it's a specific location within a defined area. Reads as "something is here, being tracked, within this boundary." Like a pin on a floorplan. The asymmetry creates visual tension and makes the mark more memorable. Risk: might feel unbalanced or arbitrary at small sizes.

Assessment

Concept Summary

  • A — Perimeter + Node: Boundary with monitored gap. Distinctive shape, but gap may read as broken/unfinished at small sizes.
  • B — Registration Mark: Square frame + interior crosshair. Precision infrastructure. Strong at all sizes. Risk: crosshair has targeting/weapon connotations.
  • C — Nested Boundary: Two concentric squares. Layers of containment. Minimal, architectural. Risk: too generic — could be any UI element.
  • D — Corner Brackets: Implied boundary zone + center node. Crop marks / viewfinder feel. Strong recognition, holds well at small sizes. Risk: viewfinder → camera association.
  • E — Anchor Block: Bold inverted form — solid red with dark void. Highest contrast, most distinctive silhouette. Risk: heavy Signal Red presence.
  • F — Offset Station: Complete boundary + asymmetric node. Memorable asymmetry, reads as "located point within defined area." Risk: dot may disappear at 16px.
