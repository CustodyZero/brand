## Design Context — StationZero

### Users

**Primary: Privacy-conscious homeowners**
People who always wanted home security but refused to trade their footage for it. They are not paranoid — they are principled. They want cameras, alerts, and automation that run on their hardware, store on their drives, and answer to no one else. They interact with StationZero the way people currently interact with Ring or Nest: live feeds, notifications on their phone, device management. The difference is that everything stays theirs.

**Secondary: Regulated enterprise operators**
IT administrators and facility managers at hospitals, schools, and institutions who need documented data residency, compliance-ready infrastructure, and institutional trust signals. They deploy StationZero across multiple buildings and floors. They need a different interface — more configuration, more status, more audit — but the same product underneath.

Both audiences share one emotional thread: **ownership as reassurance.** This is serious infrastructure that protects what matters, and it is unambiguously yours.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: Grounded. Owned. Vigilant.**

- **Grounded** — this is real infrastructure, not a gadget. It sits on your hardware, in your home, under your control. The brand should feel like something permanent you installed, not something you subscribed to.
- **Owned** — the central emotional note. YOUR technology, YOUR data, YOUR feed. Not defiance — confidence. The quiet certainty of someone who owns their tools outright.
- **Vigilant** — always watching, always local, always ready. Not paranoid, not aggressive. Steady, attentive, reliable.

### Aesthetic Direction

StationZero carves its own visual identity within the CustodyZero house — distinct from Archon's sci-fi brutalism, Factory's utilitarian restraint, and Type's literary warmth. It inherits the dark background stack, Bebas Neue, and DM Mono, but everything else is its own.

**Visual tone:** Operational clarity with domestic confidence. This should feel like professional infrastructure that lives comfortably in a home. Not a military command center (too aggressive). Not a consumer gadget app (too disposable). Not a cloud dashboard (wrong ownership model). Think: a well-designed control panel in a building you own.

**Theme:** Dark — inherited from CustodyZero house foundation. Near-black backgrounds (`#0A0A0A` → `#111111` → `#1A1A1A`). No exceptions.

**Anti-references:**
- Ring / Nest / Google Home aesthetic — rounded corners, friendly pastels, cloud-first visual language. Anything that implies "we're holding your data for you" is wrong.
- Generic smart home apps — gradient blobs, device illustrations, lifestyle imagery
- Archon's sci-fi theatrics — scan lines, panel geometry, ambient glow. StationZero is not a terminal. It's a home.
- Purple anything, per CustodyZero house constraint

**References (conceptual, not visual copies):**
- The confidence of UniFi Protect's interface — professional, dense when needed, not trying to be cute
- The permanence of architectural signage — you see it and know it belongs to the building
- The honesty of well-designed industrial controls — every element means something

### Design Principles

1. **Ownership is visible.** Every surface should reinforce that this system belongs to the user. No cloud indicators, no "syncing" language, no ambiguity about where data lives. The interface says "yours" through its structure, not just its copy.

2. **Infrastructure, not gadget.** StationZero should feel like something you installed in your home, not something you downloaded. Permanent, solid, professional. The kind of thing that makes you feel your home is properly equipped.

3. **Operational, not theatrical.** Information density serves the user's need to know what's happening. But density is earned — the consumer interface is calm and clear; the enterprise interface can be denser. Neither is decorative.

4. **Domestic confidence.** This lives in someone's home. It should feel serious without feeling hostile. Vigilant without feeling paranoid. Professional without feeling institutional. The emotional register is a homeowner who is in control and knows it.

5. **Distinct within the house.** StationZero is not Archon with a different color. It has its own visual character, earned by serving a fundamentally different context — physical spaces, cameras, homes, families — than any other CustodyZero product.

### Existing Design System Constraints

- Dark background stack: `#0A0A0A` → `#111111` → `#1A1A1A`
- Display type: Bebas Neue (uppercase, tight tracking)
- UI type: DM Mono (navigation, labels, CTAs)
- House accent: Amber `#D4880A` (subordinate in StationZero contexts)
- No purple. No border-radius on primary interactive elements. No stock imagery. No emojis.
- Square edges required. No Inter/Roboto/system fonts.
- Wordmark: "Station" in `--white` (`#F2F2EC`), "Zero" in StationZero accent color
- Icon mark: 64x64 canvas, no surveillance camera/eye/lens imagery
- Sibling accents to avoid conflict with: Archon blue `#4FC3F7`, Factory green `#5A9A6E`

## Design Context — Valet / Steward

### Users

Individuals who use cloud-AI tools daily (Claude, ChatGPT, Cowork) and chafe at their cloud-tenancy, metering, session-bounded memory, and data extraction. Hardware-capable (Apple Silicon or capable consumer GPU). Values continuity of memory and sovereignty over convenience of scale. Not the enterprise, not the developer specifically — the individual who wants a **lifelong local intelligence** that lives on their own silicon and acts through their own substrate (Drive, iCloud, OneDrive).

### Job to be done

Replace the cloud-AI assistant with something that lives on their hardware, knows them over time, and acts across their own tools. Continuous, personalized, unlimited inference — without the "agent that does X for you" builder-economy framing.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: Discrete. Attentive. Enduring.**

- **Discrete** — present but not forward. Knows but doesn't overshare. No theatrics, no announcement, no sparkle. The classical valet's virtue.
- **Attentive** — always at hand. Responsive, steady, continuous. The dignity of competent service, not the servility.
- **Enduring** — lifelong relationship. Continuity of memory and presence. A valet is not a session; a steward holds a domain across time.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Not consumer-AI (gradient orbs, glowing sparkles). Not futuristic (chrome, neon, sci-fi panel). Not military (command center, ops grid).

Register: *hand-bound leather, tarnished brass, felt-lined wood, engraved type on a door plate, the polished silver of a well-tended service set*. Not literal textures — the discipline those materials carry: tactile warmth, restraint, permanence, refined quiet. **Classical but rendered in contemporary geometry**; no faux-Victorian cliché.

**Theme:** Dark — inherited from house foundation.

**Anti-references:**
- Martin (YC) "personal AI Butler" brand
- Private Mind's app-store visual language
- Apple / Google "Personal Intelligence" marketing
- Consumer AI aesthetics: gradient orbs, anthropomorphic blobs, sparkle iconography
- Agent dispatch interfaces, autonomous-robot visual metaphors
- Purple anything (house constraint)

**References (conceptual, not visual copies):**
- Gentleman's valet box: discrete, organized, purposeful
- Leather-bound ledger: continuity and trust as physical objects
- Engraved brass name plate: permanent, refined, quiet authority
- Hotel concierge desk lamp: attentive presence
- Well-tended library: everything in its place, always at hand

### Design Principles

1. **Presence without theatrics.** The mark is there; it does not demand attention.
2. **Classical, not nostalgic.** Contemporary geometry carrying classical-register discipline.
3. **Resident, not visiting.** Every surface reinforces that this lives with you, not that it visits from the cloud.
4. **Personal, not institutional.** Where Archon is architectural and Factory is industrial, Valet/Steward is **personal** — intimate in register, refined in execution.
5. **Intentional material language.** Each CustodyZero product earns its material register (Type: paper/ink; Factory: steel; Archon: concrete; StationZero: operational; Valet: *bound leather, brass, felt*).

### Existing Design System Constraints

- Dark background stack: `#0A0A0A` → `#111111` → `#1A1A1A`
- Display type: Bebas Neue (uppercase, tight tracking)
- UI type: DM Mono (navigation, labels, CTAs)
- **Product voice serif — LOCKED:** Cardo (David J. Perry, transitional / scholar's)
  - Google Fonts: `Cardo:ital,wght@0,400;0,700;1,400`
  - Weights in use: 400 regular · 700 bold · 400 italic
  - Role: body prose, pull quotes, tagline copy, inline emphasis — everywhere a warmer voice than DM Mono is needed
  - Register: formal · restrained · academic-classical · most distinct from Type's Fraunces
- House accent: Amber `#D4880A` (subordinate in Valet contexts)
- **Product accent — LOCKED:** Bronze B·04 `#9D7E49` · `oklch(0.54 0.075 82)` — lifted bronze, reads as *brass still in current service*
- **Product accent dim — LOCKED:** `#5C4A2D` · `oklch(0.34 0.050 82)` — paired reduced-emphasis tone for tags, quiet borders, inactive state
- Single-token system (no two-anchor split required — B·04 clears WCAG UI-component 3:1 on all house backgrounds; clears 4.5:1 on #0A0A0A and #111111)
- **Icon mark — LOCKED:** I·02 a·03 "The Binding" (thickened center)
  - Top bar: `x=22, y=20, x2=42` — stroke 2.5, width 20 px
  - Center bar: `x=14, y=32, x2=50` — stroke 3.5, width 36 px (accent bar, thicker)
  - Bottom bar: `x=22, y=44, x2=42` — stroke 2.5, width 20 px
  - 64×64 viewBox, stroke-linecap square, color B·04 bronze
  - Register: bookbinding marks on a spine — continuity, record, enduring form
- **Icon motion (thinking state) — LOCKED:** M·01 "Sweep"
  - Sequential bar opacity top → center → bottom
  - Opacity range 0.40 → 1.00, staggered delays 0s / 0.4s / 0.8s
  - Duration 2.4s for thinking (ambient) · 1.6–1.8s for busy (active work)
  - Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.37, 0, 0.63, 1)` (ease-tide)
  - Animates `opacity` only (GPU-accelerated; no layout)
  - Respects `prefers-reduced-motion` (falls back to static full-opacity)
- No purple. No border-radius on primary interactive elements. No stock imagery. No emojis. Square edges required. No Inter/Roboto/system fonts.
- Icon mark: 64x64 canvas, no service-worker iconography (no bell, no key, no tray, no keyhole)
- Sibling accents to avoid conflict with: Archon blue `#4FC3F7`, Factory green `#5A9A6E`, Type pencil `#8B3A3A`, StationZero signal red `#C04848`, House amber `#D4880A`
- Parallel deliverable: Steward is pre-vetted fallback; all design decisions (color, icon, voice serif, guidelines) apply identically to both Valet and Steward — only wordmark text differs
