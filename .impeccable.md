## Design Context

### Users

**Primary: Privacy-conscious homeowners**
People who always wanted home security but refused to trade their footage for it. They are not paranoid — they are principled. They want cameras, alerts, and automation that run on their hardware, store on their drives, and answer to no one else. They interact with StationZero the way people currently interact with Ring or Nest: live feeds, notifications on their phone, device management. The difference is that everything stays theirs.

**Secondary: Regulated enterprise operators**
IT administrators and facility managers at hospitals, schools, and institutions who need documented data residency, compliance-ready infrastructure, and institutional trust signals. They deploy StationZero across multiple buildings and floors. They need a different interface — more configuration, more status, more audit — but the same product underneath.

Both audiences share one emotional thread: **ownership as reassurance.** This is serious infrastructure that protects what matters, and it is unambiguously yours.

### Brand Personality

**Three words: Grounded. Owned. Vigilant.**

- **Grounded** — this is real infrastructure, not a gadget. It sits on your hardware, in your home, under your control. The brand should feel like something permanent you installed, not something you subscribed to.
- **Owned** — the central emotional note. YOUR technology, YOUR data, YOUR feed. Not defiance — confidence. The quiet certainty of someone who owns their tools outright.
- **Vigilant** — always watching, always local, always ready. Not paranoid, not aggressive. Steady, attentive, reliable.

### Aesthetic Direction

StationZero carves its own visual identity within the CustodyZero house — distinct from Archon's sci-fi brutalism, Factory's utilitarian restraint, and Type's literary warmth. It inherits the dark background stack, Bebas Neue, and DM Mono, but everything else is its own.

**Visual tone:** Operational clarity with domestic confidence. This should feel like professional infrastructure that lives comfortably in a home. Not a military command center (too aggressive). Not a consumer gadget app (too disposable). Not a cloud dashboard (wrong ownership model). Think: a well-designed control panel in a building you own.

**Theme:** Dark — inherited from CustodyZero house foundation. Near-black backgrounds (`#0A0A0A` → `#111111` → `#1A1A1A`). No exceptions.

**Anti-references:**
- Ring / Nest / Google Home aesthetic — rounded corners, friendly pastels, cloud-first visual language. Anything that implies "we're holding your data for you" is wrong.
- Generic smart home apps — gradient blobs, device illustrations, lifestyle imagery
- Archon's sci-fi theatrics — scan lines, panel geometry, ambient glow. StationZero is not a terminal. It's a home.
- Purple anything, per CustodyZero house constraint

**References (conceptual, not visual copies):**
- The confidence of UniFi Protect's interface — professional, dense when needed, not trying to be cute
- The permanence of architectural signage — you see it and know it belongs to the building
- The honesty of well-designed industrial controls — every element means something

### Design Principles

1. **Ownership is visible.** Every surface should reinforce that this system belongs to the user. No cloud indicators, no "syncing" language, no ambiguity about where data lives. The interface says "yours" through its structure, not just its copy.

2. **Infrastructure, not gadget.** StationZero should feel like something you installed in your home, not something you downloaded. Permanent, solid, professional. The kind of thing that makes you feel your home is properly equipped.

3. **Operational, not theatrical.** Information density serves the user's need to know what's happening. But density is earned — the consumer interface is calm and clear; the enterprise interface can be denser. Neither is decorative.

4. **Domestic confidence.** This lives in someone's home. It should feel serious without feeling hostile. Vigilant without feeling paranoid. Professional without feeling institutional. The emotional register is a homeowner who is in control and knows it.

5. **Distinct within the house.** StationZero is not Archon with a different color. It has its own visual character, earned by serving a fundamentally different context — physical spaces, cameras, homes, families — than any other CustodyZero product.

### Existing Design System Constraints

- Dark background stack: `#0A0A0A` → `#111111` → `#1A1A1A`
- Display type: Bebas Neue (uppercase, tight tracking)
- UI type: DM Mono (navigation, labels, CTAs)
- House accent: Amber `#D4880A` (subordinate in StationZero contexts)
- No purple. No border-radius on primary interactive elements. No stock imagery. No emojis.
- Square edges required. No Inter/Roboto/system fonts.
- Wordmark: "Station" in `--white` (`#F2F2EC`), "Zero" in StationZero accent color
- Icon mark: 64x64 canvas, no surveillance camera/eye/lens imagery
- Sibling accents to avoid conflict with: Archon blue `#4FC3F7`, Factory green `#5A9A6E`
